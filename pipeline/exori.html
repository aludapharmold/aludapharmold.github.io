Exosome Release Inhibitors (ExoRI)

Exosomes are increasingly recognized as a central mediator in cancer formation, progression, metastasis, and drug resistance. Small vesicles produced by and shed from cancer cells in highly elevated amounts, exosomes carry out, or influence, almost all pathogenic actions of tumor cells:

  • Transport enriched and cancer-specific materials to other cells (proteins, mRNAs, ncRNAs, etc.)
  • Establish the Tumor Micro-Environment (TME) and determine how and where cancer spreads
  • Allow cancer cells to evade immune surveillance by promoting Regulatory T cells (Tregs), M2 macrophages, and myeloid-derived suppressor cells (MDSCs), and suppressing effector T cells, NK cells and dendritic cells

Inhibition of exosomes appears to offer a promising and completely novel intervention in cancer, and one that is relatively untapped due to the technical challenges of effectively intervening in the complex biology of exosomes, across a life cycle of creation, trafficking to the cell surface, and release through the cell membrane. Aluda has identified effective ways to use small molecules to intervene in this life cycle, or biogenesis. The company has generated in vitro and in vivo evidence that its compounds inhibit release of exosomes from multiple types of cells, including visualizations of cancer cells showing altered shedding patterns (images and video available). Both negative effects on tumors, and positive effects on normal cells (resistance to transformation, restoration of immune response, other), have been assessed.

We refer to these compounds as Exosome Release Inhibitors (ExoRIs). These highly novel data combined with the broad published research on the roles of exosomes, combine to make strong evidence and validation for clinical development of an ExoRI in cancer as an anti-metastatic agent as well as numerous other indications. Aluda’s lead compound is in IND enabling studies in 2019, with potential to initiate clinical studies in 2020. Data available under NDA.
